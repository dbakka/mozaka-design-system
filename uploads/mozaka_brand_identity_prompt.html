Brand Identity System — Master Prompt

Mozaka
Global Afrohouse Enterprise

A complete creative brief for building the Mozaka brand universe — music, community, events, and culture.

01 — Brand Foundation

Brand Essence

Mozaka is the sonic bridge between ancient African heritage and the global electronic dancefloor. Born in Uganda, built for the world. Every design decision must feel like it was made at the intersection of a Kampala night market and a Berlin warehouse rave.

Brand Positioning Statement

"For the global Afrohouse generation — Mozaka is the enterprise that turns African cultural DNA into the world's most compelling electronic music, events, and community."

Brand Personality

Raw Cultural Electric Bold Community-first Global Rooted

02 — Visual Identity

Color System

Mozaka Orange

Deep Black

Bone White

Neiwe Yellow

Deep Purple

Savanna Green

Orange is always dominant. Black and bone white are the structural neutrals. Secondary colors (yellow, purple, green) are reserved for individual pillar identities and release art.

Typography System

Display / Mozaka Wordmark: Custom graffiti-tribal hybrid — used for the Mozaka name only. Never stretched or recolored.

Headlines: Heavy sans-serif, all caps, wide tracking (e.g. Black Han Sans, Big Shoulders Display, or similar compressed grotesque). Used for event names, pillar names, section headers.

Body / UI: Clean modern sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans). Used for descriptions, bios, facts.

Accent / Cultural: Blackletter or ornate serif for specific releases (Kakaaba, Bwakedde) to signal heritage. Used sparingly.

Pattern System — Afro-Ndebele Remix

Develop a proprietary Mozaka geometric pattern system rooted in Ndebele geometry — triangles, chevrons, concentric circles — but remixed with the Mozaka spiral motif (from the logo). Patterns come in 3 densities: full-fill, halftone, and outline. Primary colorway: orange on black. Secondary: bone on orange. Used as backgrounds, texture overlays, textile prints, and merch.

Logo Usage Rules

Primary: Mozaka wordmark (graffiti style) on white or black only. Secondary: Circular badge/seal version for merchandise and embroidery. Tertiary: "M" monogram for app icons and small formats. Never place the logo on busy photography without a solid block behind it. Minimum clear space = 1x logo height on all sides.

06 — Brand Voice & Tone

Speak like a creator, not a corporation. Mozaka's voice is confident, culturally grounded, occasionally poetic, and always community-forward. It uses African languages (Luganda, Runyankole) naturally — not as an aesthetic, but because that's who we are.

Do: "The frequency chose us. Now we're coming for the dancefloor."
Don't: "Mozaka is proud to announce our latest release."

Key tones: Bold on announcements. Warm on community. Poetic on releases. Direct on events.
