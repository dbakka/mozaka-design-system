## 1. Brand in One Paragraph

Mozaka Run Club is a Saturday morning ritual in Nairobi — a 5km run at dawn followed by a 90-minute DJ set, speed dating, and chess. It exists because loneliness is a design problem, and most solutions to loneliness are boring. We are not a race, not a wellness program, not a fitness app. We are the thing that replaces Friday night out: a reason to sleep early, wake up early, and meet the person you'll see every Saturday for the next two years. The brand is the same hand that made Mozaka — the Night Owl Sound, the Afrofuturist identity, the three-color discipline — but turned toward morning. Where Mozaka is darkness and bass, the Run Club is dawn and stride. Same universe. Different hour.

## 2. Color System

### The Palette

All seven colors and their weights:

| Token | Name | HEX | Weight | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `--dawu` | Dawu | `#E8632A` | ~40% | Primary brand color. Dawn ember orange. CTAs, primary headlines, mark color on light backgrounds. |
| `--usiku` | Usiku | `#0E0B1F` | ~25% | Deep indigo-black. Event posters, dark mode, default background. Carries the night-into-morning transition. |
| `--haraka` | Haraka | `#FF3B5C` | ~10% | Coral-red. Finish line energy, heartbeat states, high-celebration moments. |
| `--jua` | Jua | `#FFB800` | ~10% | Solar gold. The fully risen sun. Used for warmth accents and celebration highlights. |
| `--pumzi` | Pumzi | `#F5ECD7` | ~25% | Warm cream. Light background, primary text on dark. Never pure white. |
| `--ngozi` | Ngozi | `#C8A882` | ~10% | Sandy brown. Muted text, secondary UI elements, dividers. |
| `--baridi` | Baridi | `#1E2D3E` | ~15% | Deep blue-grey. Secondary dark surface. Cool morning-air tone. |
| `--volt` | Volt | `#D4F000` | ~5% | Electric chartreuse. The single accent that cuts through everything. Use sparingly and precisely. |

### Relationship to the Main Mozaka Brand

The main Mozaka identity runs on three colors — Void Black (#0A0A0A), Signal Orange (#F94B0F), Concrete Grey (#2D2D2D) — with Bone as the print surface. The Run Club palette is the sunrise counterpart to that system: where Mozaka is near-black, we are indigo; where Mozaka is harsh Signal Orange, we are the warmer, earthier Dawu. The shared instinct is the same: restraint, contrast, the avoidance of the gradient.

### Approved Pairings

**Dawn Poster** — Pumzi background, Usiku headline, Dawu CTA.
*Use for: morning-context posts, WhatsApp blasts, light-mode digital.*

**Night Poster** — Usiku background, Pumzi headline, Volt accent.
*Use for: event announcements, IG posts, after-dark content.*

**Energy Burst** — Dawu background, Pumzi headline, Volt one word.
*Use for: finish line posts, celebrations, milestone announcements.*

**Data Card** — Baridi background, Pumzi text, Jua highlight.
*Use for: run stats, pace cards, participant counts.*

### The Volt Rule

Volt (#D4F000) is used once per composition, maximum. It is a pointer, not a field. Use it on: the solo mark on a dark vest, one word in an eight-word headline, the dot after "MOZAKA RUN CLUB" on a poster, the wristband print. When everything is volt, nothing is.

## 3. Typography System

### The Three Families

**Big Shoulders Display** (Google Fonts) → *Display*
Ultra-condensed, bold, upright. The brand's headline voice. Shares the same commanding stature as Monument Extended (the main Mozaka font) — making the Run Club feel like a family member, not a stranger. Where Monument is industrial-night, Big Shoulders is athletic-dawn.

**DM Sans** (Google Fonts) → *Body*
Geometric, warm, and built for screens. The voice Mozaka Run Club uses when talking to you, not at you. Roles: body copy, captions, WhatsApp blasts, social captions, UI labels.

**Space Mono** (Google Fonts) → *Data*
Technical, precise, cool. Used only for quantitative content: pace, distance, time, participant count, dates. `5:32/km` in Space Mono feels earned. In DM Sans it feels casual. The distinction is the brand.

### Type Scale

| Role | Family | Weight | Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero | Big Shoulders Display | 900 | clamp(64px, 9vw, 120px) |
| H1 | Big Shoulders Display | 900 | clamp(48px, 6vw, 80px) |
| H2 | Big Shoulders Display | 800 | clamp(36px, 4vw, 56px) |
| H3 | Big Shoulders Display | 700 | 32px |
| Eyebrow | DM Sans | 700 (ALL CAPS) | 12px, +0.12em tracking |
| Body | DM Sans | 400 | 18px |
| Caption | DM Sans | 400 | 14px |
| Data / Mono | Space Mono | 400 | 14–18px |

### Do / Don't

**Do:** Set Big Shoulders Display headlines in all-caps with -0.02em tracking. The condensed geometry demands it.

**Don't:** Use Big Shoulders Display for body copy, captions, or anything that runs more than one line at under 32px. It is a headline instrument.

**Do:** Use Space Mono for every pace, time, and numerical stat.

**Don't:** Mix Space Mono and Big Shoulders Display in the same text block. They serve different registers and will fight.

**Do:** Set DM Sans body at 18px / 1.6 line-height on all screens. Mozaka copy is read on phones at 7am post-run.

**Don't:** Use DM Sans 300 (thin) for anything that sits on dark backgrounds. The weight disappears.

## 4. Logo Usage

See `logo-concept.md` for full design brief. Usage rules here:

**Clear space:** On all four sides of the full lockup, clear space equals the cap-height of the "M" in MOZAKA. Nothing — type, image, edge of frame — enters that zone.

**Minimum sizes:**
- Full lockup: 120px wide digital / 28mm print
- Solo mark: 24px digital / 10mm print
- Monogram MRC: 16px digital / 6mm print

**Color precedence:**
1. Dawu mark + Usiku wordmark on Pumzi (morning default)
2. Volt mark + Pumzi wordmark on Usiku (dark default, event poster)
3. Pumzi mark + Pumzi wordmark on Dawu (energy burst)

**Sizing on social:**
- Instagram post: mark at ~12% of canvas width in the corner or full lockup centered
- Stories/Reels: monogram at top center, 8% canvas width, with the event headline below
- Profile avatar: solo mark, centered, on Usiku background

## 5. Photography Style

### Lighting

Dawn only. The golden 20 minutes before and after the 7am sun-crest is the brand's native light. Photography should be made in this window — not studio-replicated after the fact. Key light from the horizon: warm amber, long shadows, halos on silhouetted runners. If the event is captured in afternoon or indoor light, grade toward this look: push the highlights toward Jua (#FFB800), pull the shadows toward Usiku (#0E0B1F), warm the midtones with Dawu.

For the after-party/DJ-set portion: practical light only — speaker rigs, a single warm bulb, phone flashlights, the sun if it's fully up. No ring lights. No studio flash used outdoors.

### Subjects

Candid, always. The camera catches people arriving, finishing, laughing mid-run, checking their watch, talking to strangers. Never posed group shots unless it's the deliberate "finish line herd" format (everyone crowded in, sweaty, mid-laugh, no one standing straight). Faces at the finish line. Hands accepting water cups. Feet crossing the line. The DJ's hands on the decks during the set. Chess boards mid-game. Eye contact between strangers who just met. The city in the background.

Diversity is not a casting note — it's an accurate description of who comes. Don't over-select. Shoot the actual room.

### Post-Processing

**Run Club LUT:** Lifted blacks (never crushed below Baridi #1E2D3E), warm midtones pushed toward Dawu, cream highlights, fine grain at ISO 400 equivalent. Skin tones rich and warm. Saturation is present but not pumped — the actual sunrise provides enough.

**After-Party LUT:** Slightly more contrast. Shadows deeper. Highlights kept warm. Grain increased to ISO 800 equivalent. The mood shifts from open to intimate — same event, different register.

Apply grain in post if it isn't in capture. A perfectly clean frame is a brand misfire.

## 6. Social Templates

### Format 1 — Weekly Event Post (1:1 or 4:5)

**Structure:** Usiku full-bleed background. The Striding Sun mark at top-left (25% canvas width) in Volt. Big Shoulders Display 900 in Pumzi: the session number ("RUN CLUB 022") at top in eyebrow scale (12px, all-caps, +0.12em tracking). Full-width headline: two lines maximum, one word each if possible ("SATURDAY / 7AM"). Below: DM Sans 400 in Ngozi for the address or "RSVP for location." Dawu orange hairline (2px) running full-width between the headline and address block.

Caption: The WhatsApp blast format, adapted — under 80 words, no hashtag spam (three max), ends with "See you at dawn."

### Format 2 — Finish Line Photo (4:5)

**Structure:** Full-bleed photography from the finish line (dawn LUT applied). No color overlay. Single text block bottom-center: participant count in Space Mono 400 Volt ("039 RUNNERS") and the session number in DM Sans 400 Pumzi below it. The solo mark in Volt at bottom-right, 64px from each edge. Nothing else.

Caption: The finish line caption format from brand-voice.md. Ends with the next session date.

### Format 3 — Runner Spotlight / Story

**Structure:** A 4:5 portrait of one runner (candid, dawn light, never posed). Pumzi scrim gradient at the bottom (0% opacity at image center, 70% at bottom). Over the scrim: Big Shoulders Display 700 headline in Dawu — their first name only. DM Sans 400 Pumzi, 14px: one sentence about why they come (written in their voice, from a WhatsApp interview). Space Mono below: their fastest 5km pace and sessions attended count.

Caption: Third person, warm, specific. "Wanjiru started coming in January. She brought two people. This is her seventh run."

### Format 4 — Partner Feature

**Structure:** Split composition. Left 40%: Pumzi field with the Run Club logo lockup and the partner's logo below it (same weight treatment, equal size, clear-space gap between them). Right 60%: a product/event photograph in dawn LUT. Big Shoulders Display 800 headline in Dawu over the right panel — one phrase. DM Sans 400 Pumzi caption below.

Tone: warm but specific. Never "we're excited to partner with." Instead: "Red Bull at the finish line. Because earned tastes different."

## 7. Merch Guidelines

**Running Vest (Primary)**
Recycled-poly performance fabric. Mesh side panels. Front: Striding Sun solo mark at heart position, Volt heat-transfer on Usiku (dark) or Dawu heat-transfer on Pumzi (cream). Back: "MOZAKA RUN CLUB" in Big Shoulders Display 900, all-caps, arched over the shoulder line in the accent color. Bottom hem: session number (e.g., "022") in Space Mono 12px — specific to each event for collector's value. Colors: Usiku (primary), Pumzi (seasonal).

**T-Shirt**
Heavyweight 240gsm cotton, boxy cut. Front: solo mark centered at 25% chest width. Back: "EARNED AT 7AM" in Big Shoulders Display 900, large, across the full back in Dawu (on Usiku) or Usiku (on Pumzi). No other text, no URLs, no years. The shirt should be wearable in five years without explanation.

**Socks**
Mid-calf, ribbed cotton. Usiku base. Single Dawu stripe across the cuff. Solo mark embroidered at the ankle in Volt thread. This is the Mozaka main-brand "Voltage Sock" translated to the run club register — different color, same obsessive detail.

**Sunglasses**
Acetate frames in Baridi (dark blue-grey) with warm-amber gradient lenses. Inside left temple: "MRC" laser-etched. Not a logoware piece — a product that stands on its own. Worn at the run, visible at the after-party, still wearable at dinner.

**Water Bottle / Cup**
Matte Usiku exterior. Volt solo mark embossed at the center. Dawu interior. No wordmark — the mark alone speaks. For event paper cups: Pumzi cup, Dawu mark at 50% cup height.

## 8. What Makes Us Different

**Different from every other Nairobi run club:**
Every other Nairobi run club has sponsors in the name, a charity hook, and a Facebook page last updated in 2023. They reward participation with T-shirts and finish-line photos where everyone stands in a row and smiles. We reward participation with a DJ set and a chess board and the specific, difficult-to-manufacture experience of meeting someone new because you both did something physical together at 7am. The difference isn't the run — the run is the entrance fee. The difference is what happens after. Most run clubs are about the run. We are about the 90 minutes after the run when strangers become regulars.

**Different from every fitness app:**
A fitness app is a mirror. It shows you your pace, your calories, your streak. The brand is transactional: do this, get that. Mozaka Run Club is a room. The brand is relational: come here, meet these people. We do not track your fitness. We do not celebrate your personal bests unless they happen to coincide with what's happening in the room. We celebrate the person who showed up for the first time and finished. We celebrate the person who brought someone new. The metric we care about is not kilometers logged — it is loneliness reduced.

**Different from every wellness brand:**
Wellness brands promise transformation. New you. Better life. Journey. We promise one specific Saturday morning. We make no claim about what the twelfth Saturday will feel like based on the first one. We say: come once, you'll see. The irreverence in the voice — the "bring your legs and your patience," the "half of you didn't think you were coming" — is the rejection of the wellness brand's core proposition, which is that you need to be fixed. You don't need to be fixed. You need to show up at dawn and run 5km with 38 other people and then dance until 9am. That's the whole thing. Come find out.
