# Mozaka Run Club — Brand Voice Guide

---

## Personality

Five words. Each is a choice, not a description.

**1. Magnetic**
People don't get invited to Mozaka Run Club — they get pulled. The voice creates desire without explaining itself. It says *come* more than it says *why*.

**2. Grounded**
Nairobi isn't backdrop. It's the subject. The heat, the matatu pace, the pre-dawn quiet of a city that hasn't started yet — the voice names these things without romanticizing them. We write *for* Nairobi, not *about* it.

**3. Irreverent**
We are allergic to corporate wellness speak. "Fitness journey." "Lifestyle goals." "Join our community." These phrases are funeral notices for a brand. Our voice is sly, a little boastful, occasionally absurd — because the premise (running at 6am followed by a DJ set) already is.

**4. Warm**
The voice is the friend who texts you the night before, not the app that sends you a push notification. It remembers your name. It asks if you finished. It is not performatively positive — it is specifically, personally, warm.

**5. Kinetic**
Short sentences. Present tense. The voice is always in motion. It does not explain at length what it can show in a line.

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Event invite** | Conspiratorial. Like being let in on something. | *Saturday, 7am. You know the spot. Bring your legs and your patience.* |
| **Finish line post** | Unfiltered pride. No corporate congratulations. | *47 humans. One finish line. Half of you didn't think you were coming. All of you showed up.* |
| **Motivation message** | Tough love. Direct. No fluff. | *The hardest part is lacing up. Everything after that is just running.* |
| **Partner collab** | Warm but precise. A cool introduction, not a press release. | *We put Red Bull next to the finish line because they understand something we do: earned tastes better.* |
| **Onboarding DM** | Personal. Specific. The first text from a friend. | *You're in. Saturday. 6:45am warm-up, 7am push. Don't be that person still stretching at 7:05.* |
| **Merch copy** | Wry and confident. Slightly boastful about nothing obvious. | *You've run 5km in this. You've danced in this. You've made a friend in this. It's seen things.* |

## Signature Phrases

Six phrases, production-ready. Use across posts, shirts, event pages, and the back of a receipt.

1. **"Run. Dance. Chess."** — The holy trinity. The tagline. Always in that order.
2. **"The best party you almost missed."** — The core pitch. Use on event pages and pre-launch hooks.
3. **"The sun is already up. Are you?"** — Morning call. WhatsApp blast closer. Shirt back.
4. **"Earned at 7am."** — Three words that do everything. Merch copy. Finish line caption. Water bottle.
5. **"47 strangers became 47 regulars."** — Adjust the number each week. For finish line posts and community milestones.
6. **"You don't have to love running. You just have to love what follows."** — Onboarding hook, story copy, and the answer to every "why should I come."

### Bi-Weekly Runners' Bible Message

> You're going to skip it.
>
> Not this week, maybe — but at some point, you'll wake up at 6am and it'll be raining, or your legs will ache, or you'll just not feel like it. And you'll decide that one week off won't hurt.
>
> Here's what you lose when you skip: nothing physical. Your 5km will still be there.
>
> What you lose is the moment at kilometer 4 where the sun comes fully over the ridge and someone next to you — a stranger three Saturdays ago — says something ridiculous and you both laugh while running. That exact moment doesn't come back. It moves to next Saturday and it happens to whoever shows up.
>
> We run because the city is different at 7am. We run because earned caffeine hits different. We run because 90 minutes from now you'll be dancing, and you'll be glad you came.
>
> Saturday. 7am. See you at the front.

---

### "Why You Should Come" Story Hook — Pre-Launch Content

> There is a specific hour in Nairobi that most people miss.
>
> It's the hour after dawn, before the matatus find their rhythm, when the streets still belong to the runners, the vendors arranging their stalls, and the pigeons who haven't been scared off yet.
>
> We built a run club around that hour. Not a race. Not a wellness program. A ritual.
>
> You run 5km. Then a DJ plays for 90 minutes. Then there's chess. Then there's speed dating. Then you go home and you have already done the most interesting thing that will happen to you this week — and it's still morning.
>
> The first time you come, you'll be skeptical. The second time, you'll bring someone. That's the whole strategy.
>
> Mozaka Run Club. Every Saturday. 7am. Run. Dance. Chess.
